What is Degrees to Radians Conversion?

The two units of measurement for angles are degrees and radians. We can convert between degrees and radians according to our convenience. This can be done using basic calculations. Before talking about Converting Degrees to Radians let us discuss the basic definition of Degrees, Radians.

Degree: It is a unit for measuring angle and is also known as the degree of arc. The symbol used to denote degrees is (°). For a complete rotation, it will be 360°. We can use a protractor to measure degrees.

Radians: Radian is also another unit used to measure the angle. Radian is the angle that is formed at the center of a circle by an arc and its radius is r. One complete counterclockwise rotation is equal to 2π Radians.

Degree to Radians Formula

You can use this generalized formula to convert degrees to radians easily. To convert degrees to radians multiply the values of the degree with π / 180. The formula for Degrees to Radians Conversion is given by

Radians = Degrees x π / 180

Worked Out Examples on Degree to Radian Conversion

Example 1:

Convert 60° to Radians?

Solution:

Given Degree = 60°

The formula to convert Degrees to Radians is given by Radian = Degree x π / 180

= 60° x π / 180

= π/3

Therefore, 60° to Radians is π/3.
