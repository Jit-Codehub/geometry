Radian & Degree Definitions

The definition of radian and degree are as follows:

Radian:It is the SI unit of measuring angles and is represented as rad. We know that one complete rotation around a circle is 2π radians. Also, it is equal to 360° degrees.

Degree:Degree is defined as a part of a plane angle in which one full rotation is 360 degrees. It is also called a degree of arc, arc degree, or arcdegree and indicated by ° or deg. The unit system of degree is not the SI unit measurement but it is an accepted unit to measure.

Radians To Degrees Conversion Formula

The formula to convert radians to degrees is equivalent to the angle α in radians times 180 degrees divided by the pi constant. It is expressed as

α(degrees) = α(radians) × 180° / π

or

degrees = radians × 180° / π
