What are Supplementary Angles?

The word supplementary describes a special relationship between two angles. The supplementray angles defination says that the sum of two angles is equal to 180° or π. In other words, if the the adjacent angles form a straight line, then are supplemenatry angles.

Two angles are called supplemenatry angles when

  • One of its angle is an acute angle and other is an obtuse nagle.
  • Both angles are right angles

Properties of Supplementary Angles

  • The two angles are called supplemenatary angles only when their sum is 180°.
  • The two angles together make a straight line, but angles need to be together.
  • The letter "S" in supplementary angles stands for "Straight line".
